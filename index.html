About me

I am a master geology student with a passion for the natural world. This means that I like to talk about things like rock formations and volcanology. I have always been fascinated by the way the Earth's processes shape the landscape and how the ocean interacts with it. I think it is important to advocate for sustainability. I also have an interest in technology and the way it is evolving with time. I like to tinker with Linux systems and certain gadgets. I am always eager to learn and explore new ideas.

Currently I am doing research about the Great Oxidation Event (GOE). This is a term used to describe a significant increase in the level of oxygen in the Earth's atmosphere that occurred around 2.4 billion years ago. Prior to the GOE, the Earth's atmosphere contained little to no free oxygen. The GOE also had a significant impact on the Earth's climate and geology, causing changes in weather patterns, and the formation of new minerals. The rise of oxygen levels also allowed for the evolution of new life forms.
